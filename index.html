Human-in-control automation

“The agent can do the boring work. The user still approves the scary parts.”

Nombo can inspect, summarize, prepare, and simulate. Signing, bridging, deposits, withdrawals, and live trades remain gated by wallet policy and explicit human approval.

Workflow

From chat instruction to guarded execution.

The commercial idea is not “let a bot gamble.” It is an operating layer that makes wallet work understandable, auditable, and safer.

1

Monitor

Read balances, policies, markets, pending approvals, and venue state without moving value.

2

Research

Scan markets, opportunities, risks, and wallet history. Produce plain-English recommendations.

3

Simulate

Paper-trade first. Track pretend entries, exits, PnL, and bad behavior before live money.

4

Approve

Prepare real actions only after rules are met, then route through MetaMask Guard Mode and MFA.

Safety

Four layers of guardrails.

Nombo should be useful without becoming reckless. The safety design treats wallet autonomy as bounded, observable, and reversible.

Nombo rules

Max position size, max leverage, stop after losses, paper-first mode, no unsupported markets.

Wallet policy

Outflow caps, allowlists/blocklists, chain restrictions, signing policy, and approvals.

Venue limits

Margin, liquidation, order rules, deposit assets, and exchange-specific risk controls.

Funding cap

The simplest protection: only fund the agent wallet with what the user is prepared to risk.
